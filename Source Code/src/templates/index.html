Introduction

Sentiment Analysis (SA) has progressed from simple binary polarity detection to multilingual and cross-domain understanding. Real-world text differs vastly across languages, topics, and contextsâ€”posing challenges for model generalization. Although transformer models like BERT, RoBERTa, DistilBERT, and XLM-RoBERTa have revolutionized SA, they still struggle with domain and language variability. Traditional ensemble techniques such as majority voting or averaging fail to capture inter-model dependencies. To address this, we propose a Meta-Fusion MLP Ensemble that dynamically learns how to combine multiple transformer outputs for robust, multilingual sentiment prediction.

Problem Statement

Existing sentiment models
  • Perform well on individual domains but fail under cross-domain or multilingual conditions.
  • Depend on static fusion techniques (voting or averaging), which ignore inter-model interactions.
There is a need for
  • A trainable fusion mechanism that learns dependencies between model logits.
  • A generalized ensemble framework effective across multiple datasets, languages, and sentiment types.

Objectives

Develop a Meta-Fusion Ensemble integrating BERT, RoBERTa, DistilBERT, and XLM-RoBERTa.

Achieve robust sentiment prediction across English and multilingual datasets, improving domain transfer and adaptability.

Enhance neutral sentiment detection, which remains a key challenge in SA.

Overcome research gaps related to noise robustness, low-resource adaptability, and domain transfer.

Enhance neutral sentiment detection and validate results through balanced evaluation (2,700 samples) for reliability.
